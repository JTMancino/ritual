Bestiary

Animals

Horse

A domestic equine creature which can be mounted in and out of combat.

Horses can carry one rider and either fifty pounds of gear or an unconscious person.

Property Value
Hit Points 8
Armor 30
Weight 1,100
Attack Damage 1d4

Wolf

A canine creature which stalks the night and hunts in packs.

Wolves do one additional damage if another wolf has hit in the last five seconds.

Property Value
Hit Points 4
Armor 50
Weight 100
Attack Damage 1d6

Hawk

An avian creature which flies at high speeds and delivers death from above.

Hawks can fly at up to 300 feet per five seconds, and be trained to deliver messages.

Property Value
Hit Points 3
Armor 70
Weight 3
Attack Damage 1d4

Snake

A vemomous reptile that often hides itself amongst the terrain.

When snakes hit, their rolled damage is from poison and the plus one is their fangs.

Property Value
Hit Points 3
Armor 40
Weight 3
Attack Damage 2d4+1

Monsters

Changeling

Changelings are small humanoid creatures with very little intelligence and a sadistic quality.

Changelings horde shiny things, and can alter their appearance though their size stays the same and they can not mimic any specific individual.

Property Value
Hit Points 10
Armor 40
Weight 60
Attack Damage 1d6

Undead

Taking the form of skeletons, ghouls, and zombies, the most basic forms of mindless undead are all the same. These are thought to be failed experiments of mystic arcanists.

Property Value
Hit Points 12
Armor 30
Weight 100
Attack Damage 1d4+1

Skin Walker

Skin Walkers are the result of druidic experiments in creating more spawn. They are lanky frankenstein amalgamations of bits and pieces from wild animals.

Many skin walkers modify their own bodies with trophies from their kills, sometimes intentionally disguising themselves as normal animals.

Property Value
Hit Points 16
Armor 60
Weight Varies
Attack Damage 1d8+2

Ravager

Ravagers are ten legged beastly creatures with four arms extending out from their body. Their heads are not located on the front of their body but in the middle, leaving room for a collection of tentacles in the front used to hold prey.

A ravager can use a special action to restrain a creature within five feet of it, preventing it from taking the move action.

Property Value
Hit Points 22
Armor 40
Weight 1,500
Attack Damage 3d4

Will-O-Wisp

Will-O-Wisps are spectral entities which take the form of those whom are most beloved to their prey. They often lead people to walk into hazards promising safety, but sometimes will simply attack.

A Will-O-Wisp can take the form of a person from a humanoid's memories, though close inspection will reveal something is slightly off. Their natural form is a small emanation of haunting blue light.

Incorporeal: Will-O-Wisps take half minus one damage from nonmagical means.

Property Value
Hit Points 10
Armor 50
Weight None
Attack Damage 2d4
